Oviraptorosauria

Oviraptorosauria is a group of feathered theropod dinosaurs that lived from the Late Jurassic to the Late Cretaceous. They are characterized by toothless beaks, crested skulls, short tails, and likely omnivorous diets. Fossils have been found mainly in Asia and North America.

Featured — Oviraptor

The most famous oviraptorosaur; known for its crest, toothless beak, and potential egg brooding behavior.

Time: Late Cretaceous (≈ 85–70 million years ago)
Region: Asia
Diet: Omnivore / Herbivore

Typical size: Length ~2 m; Estimated mass ~20–40 kg.

Notable genera in Oviraptorosauria

Oviraptor

Small, crested theropod; famous for egg brooding fossils and toothless beak.

Citipati

Feathered oviraptorosaur from Asia; shows nesting behavior and typical oviraptorid anatomy.

Caudipteryx

Early feathered oviraptorosaur from China; displays primitive features and feathers on arms and tail.
