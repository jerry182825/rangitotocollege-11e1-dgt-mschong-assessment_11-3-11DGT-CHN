Therizinosauroidea

Therizinosauroidea is a superfamily of unusual theropod dinosaurs that lived from the Late Jurassic to the Late Cretaceous. They are characterized by long necks, large claws, broad torsos, and likely herbivorous or omnivorous diets. Fossils have been found in Asia and North America.

Featured — Therizinosaurus

The most famous therizinosauroid; known for its enormous claws, long neck, and herbivorous adaptations.

Time: Late Cretaceous (≈ 70–65 million years ago)
Region: Asia
Diet: Herbivore / Omnivore

Typical size: Length ~10 m; Estimated mass ~5 tons.

Notable genera in Therizinosauroidea

Therizinosaurus

Giant therizinosauroid with extremely long claws; herbivorous adaptations and distinctive build.

Alxasaurus

Smaller therizinosauroid from Asia; shows primitive features and early evolutionary traits.

Nothronychus

North American therizinosauroid; demonstrates diversity and broad body morphology in the group.
