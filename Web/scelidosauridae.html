Scelidosauridae

Scelidosauridae is a family of early, herbivorous, quadrupedal armored dinosaurs that lived during the Early Jurassic. They are characterized by small heads, beak-like mouths, and rows of bony armor along the back and tail. Fossils have been found mainly in Europe and North America.

Featured — Scelidosaurus

The best-known scelidosaurid; small herbivore with body armor and a beak for feeding on plants.

Time: Early Jurassic (≈ 191–183 million years ago)
Region: Europe
Diet: Herbivore

Typical size: Length ~4 m; Estimated mass ~250–300 kg.

Notable genera in Scelidosauridae

Scelidosaurus

Early armored herbivore; small head, beaked mouth, and body armor along the back.

Europasaurus

Early Jurassic herbivorous dinosaur; shows primitive armor and quadrupedal posture.

Callovosaurus

Small herbivorous dinosaur; considered closely related to scelidosaurids with early armor development.
