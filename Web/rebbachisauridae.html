Rebbachisauridae

Rebbachisauridae is a family of medium-sized, herbivorous sauropods primarily from the Cretaceous. They are distinguished by elongated vertebrae, sometimes with spines or sail-like structures, and narrow teeth suitable for browsing. Fossils are mainly found in South America, Africa, and Europe.

Featured — Rebbachisaurus

A typical member of Rebbachisauridae; recognized for its elongated vertebrae and distinctive teeth.

Time: Early Cretaceous (≈ 130–125 million years ago)
Region: Africa
Diet: Herbivore / browser

Typical size: Length ~15 m; Estimated mass ~5–7 tons.

Notable genera in Rebbachisauridae

Rebbachisaurus

Medium-sized sauropod from Africa; elongated vertebrae and narrow teeth.

Nigersaurus

Specialized low-browser with wide mouth and numerous teeth; from Niger, Africa.

Limaysaurus

Cretaceous rebbachisaurid from South America; shows typical vertebral elongation.
