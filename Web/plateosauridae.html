Plateosauridae

Plateosauridae is a group of early, medium-to-large herbivorous sauropodomorph dinosaurs that lived primarily during the Late Triassic to Early Jurassic. Members of this family were generally bipedal or facultatively quadrupedal, with long necks and small heads adapted for browsing vegetation. They are among the earliest large-bodied herbivores in the dinosaur fossil record and help illustrate the transition toward the giant sauropods that would dominate later Mesozoic ecosystems.

Notable genera in/near Plateosauridae

Plateosaurus

A well-known Late Triassic sauropodomorph from Central Europe; numerous skeletons recovered.

Sellosaurus

Sometimes treated as close to Plateosaurus; known from Triassic deposits and similar body plan.

Gresslyosaurus

A genus historically allied with Plateosaurus in older literature; illustrates taxonomic complexity of early sauropodomorphs.
