Iguanodontia

Iguanodontia is a clade of large, herbivorous, mostly bipedal to facultatively quadrupedal dinosaurs that lived from the Late Jurassic to the Late Cretaceous. They are characterized by robust bodies, specialized teeth for grinding plants, and often thumb spikes for defense. Fossils have been found mainly in Europe, North America, and Asia.

Featured — Iguanodon

The most famous iguanodontian; large herbivore with a characteristic thumb spike and strong hind limbs.

Time: Early Cretaceous (≈ 139–125 million years ago)
Region: Europe
Diet: Herbivore

Typical size: Length ~9–10 m; Estimated mass ~3–5 tons.

Notable genera in Iguanodontia

Iguanodon

Large herbivore; notable for thumb spikes, strong hind limbs, and herbivorous adaptations.

Ouranosaurus

Early Cretaceous iguanodontian from Africa; long neural spines possibly supporting a sail or hump.

Camptosaurus

Smaller iguanodontian from North America; facultatively bipedal with leaf-shaped teeth for plants.
