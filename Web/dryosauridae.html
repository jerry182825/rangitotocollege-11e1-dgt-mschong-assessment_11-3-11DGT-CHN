Dryosauridae

Dryosauridae is a family of small to medium-sized, herbivorous, bipedal dinosaurs that lived from the Late Jurassic to the Early Cretaceous. They are characterized by lightly built bodies, long legs for fast running, small beaked heads, and leaf-shaped teeth for processing plants. Fossils have been found mainly in North America, Europe, and Africa.

Featured — Dryosaurus

The most well-known dryosaurid; small, fast-running herbivore with a beaked mouth and leaf-shaped teeth.

Time: Late Jurassic (≈ 155–145 million years ago)
Region: North America
Diet: Herbivore

Typical size: Length ~3 m; Estimated mass ~100–150 kg.

Notable genera in Dryosauridae

Dryosaurus

Small, agile herbivore; lightly built body and leaf-shaped teeth for eating plants.

Valdosaurus

European dryosaurid; adapted for running with long legs and small beaked head.

Osmakasaurus

Early Cretaceous dryosaurid from Africa; shows typical dryosaurid body plan and herbivorous adaptations.
