Pachycephalosauridae

Pachycephalosauridae is a family of small to medium-sized, herbivorous or omnivorous dinosaurs that lived during the Late Cretaceous. They are characterized by thick, domed skulls, often with knobs and spikes on the head, short forelimbs, and bipedal posture. Fossils have been found mainly in North America and Asia.

Featured — Pachycephalosaurus

The most famous pachycephalosaurid; known for its thick domed skull and bipedal stance.

Time: Late Cretaceous (≈ 76–65 million years ago)
Region: North America
Diet: Herbivore / Omnivore

Typical size: Length ~4–5 m; Estimated mass ~450–550 kg.

Notable genera in Pachycephalosauridae

Pachycephalosaurus

Large domed skull; bipedal herbivore/omnivore with thick cranial roof and head ornaments.

Stegoceras

Small pachycephalosaurid; rounded skull dome with spikes and knobs on the head for display or combat.

Stygimoloch

Medium-sized pachycephalosaurid; known for spiky cranial ornaments and domed skull.
