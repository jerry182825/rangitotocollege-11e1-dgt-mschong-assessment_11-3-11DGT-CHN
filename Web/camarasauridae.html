Camarasauridae

Camarasauridae is a family of large, robust sauropod dinosaurs from the Late Jurassic. They are characterized by box-shaped skulls, strong limbs, and relatively shorter necks compared to diplodocids. Fossils are mainly found in North America and Europe.

Featured — Camarasaurus

The most representative camarasaurid; known for its boxy skull and strong, pillar-like limbs.

Time: Late Jurassic (≈ 155–145 million years ago)
Region: North America
Diet: Herbivore / browser

Typical size: Length ~18–23 m; Estimated mass ~15–20 tons.

Notable genera in Camarasauridae
