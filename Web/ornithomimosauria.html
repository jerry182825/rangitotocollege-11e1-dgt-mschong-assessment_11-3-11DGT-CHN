Ornithomimosauria

Ornithomimosauria is a group of fast, lightly built theropod dinosaurs that lived from the Late Jurassic to the Late Cretaceous. They are characterized by long legs, small heads with toothless beaks, long necks, and often feathered bodies. Fossils have been found in North America, Asia, and Europe.

Featured — Ornithomimus

The most representative ornithomimosaur; known for its speed, toothless beak, and slender build.

Time: Late Cretaceous (≈ 76–66 million years ago)
Region: North America
Diet: Omnivore / herbivore

Typical size: Length ~3–4 m; Estimated mass ~150–200 kg.

Notable genera in Ornithomimosauria

Ornithomimus

Small to medium-sized theropod; fast runner with toothless beak; fossils mainly from North America.

Struthiomimus

Slender, ostrich-like dinosaur from North America; illustrates diversity within Ornithomimosauria.

Gallimimus

Large ornithomimosaur from Asia; shows extreme adaptations for speed and agility.
