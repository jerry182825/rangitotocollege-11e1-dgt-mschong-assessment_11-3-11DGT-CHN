Diplodocidae

Diplodocidae is a family of long-necked, herbivorous sauropod dinosaurs that lived during the Late Jurassic. They are among the longest dinosaurs ever known, characterized by extremely elongated necks and tails, light but strong limb bones, and specialized peg-like teeth for stripping foliage. Fossils are mainly found in North America and Europe.

Featured — Diplodocus

The most iconic member of Diplodocidae; famous for its extremely long neck and tail.

Time: Late Jurassic (≈ 154–150 million years ago)
Region: North America
Diet: Herbivore / browser

Typical size: Length ~24–30 m; Estimated mass ~12–16 tons. Adapted for high browsing and large-scale herbivory.

Notable genera in Diplodocidae

Diplodocus

One of the longest known dinosaurs; quadrupedal, with an extremely long neck and tail; abundant fossils in North America.

Barosaurus

Closely related to Diplodocus; known for an even longer neck relative to its body.

Apatosaurus

Robust diplodocid from North America; shows more massive limbs and shorter neck than Diplodocus.
