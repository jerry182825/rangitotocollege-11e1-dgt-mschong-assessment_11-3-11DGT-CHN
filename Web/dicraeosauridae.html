Dicraeosauridae

Dicraeosauridae is a family of smaller, short-necked sauropods from the Late Jurassic and Early Cretaceous. They are notable for relatively shorter necks, often bifurcated neural spines, and a more compact body than typical diplodocids. Fossils are mainly found in South America, Africa, and North America.

Featured — Dicraeosaurus

A small, short-necked diplodocoid; famous for its compact body and bifurcated spines.

Time: Late Jurassic (≈ 155–150 million years ago)
Region: Africa
Diet: Herbivore / browser

Typical size: Length ~9–11 m; Estimated mass ~3–5 tons.

Notable genera in Dicraeosauridae

Dicraeosaurus

Compact diplodocoid with bifurcated neural spines; fossils mainly from Tanzania.

Brachytrachelopan

Short-necked dicraeosaurid from Argentina; shows extreme neck reduction relative to body size.

Amargasaurus

Known for elongated spines forming a sail or spikes along the neck and back; South America.
