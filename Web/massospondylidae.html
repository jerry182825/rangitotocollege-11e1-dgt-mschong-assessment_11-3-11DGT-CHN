Massospondylidae

Massospondylidae is a family of early, primarily herbivorous sauropodomorph dinosaurs that lived during the Late Triassic to Early Jurassic. They were generally small to medium-sized, bipedal or facultatively quadrupedal, and are important for understanding the evolutionary transition toward larger sauropods. Fossil evidence comes mainly from Africa, North America, and parts of Asia.

Featured — Massospondylus

The best-known member of Massospondylidae; often studied for growth patterns and locomotion.

Time: Late Triassic to Early Jurassic (≈ 235–183 million years ago)
Region: Africa, North America, Asia
Diet: Herbivore / browser

Typical size: Length ~4–6 m; Estimated mass ~250–1000 kg. Shows variation between juveniles and adults in limb proportions and body size.

Notable genera in/near Massospondylidae

Massospondylus

Well-studied Late Triassic/Early Jurassic dinosaur from South Africa and Lesotho; numerous skeletons known.

Coloradisaurus

From Argentina; closely related to Massospondylus and similar in size and body plan.

Ignavusaurus

A lesser-known genus; provides insight into the early diversity of sauropodomorphs.
