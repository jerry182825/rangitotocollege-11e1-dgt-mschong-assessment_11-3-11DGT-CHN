Dromaeosauridae

Dromaeosauridae is a family of feathered theropod dinosaurs that lived from the Late Jurassic to the Late Cretaceous. They are agile, carnivorous, and known for their distinctive sickle-shaped claw on the second toe. Fossils are found in North America, Europe, and Asia.

Featured — Velociraptor

One of the most famous dromaeosaurids; agile predator with feathers and a sickle-shaped claw.

Time: Late Cretaceous (≈ 75–71 million years ago)
Region: Asia
Diet: Carnivore

Typical size: Length ~2 m; Estimated mass ~15–20 kg.

Notable genera in Dromaeosauridae

Velociraptor

Small, intelligent predator with sickle-shaped claw; feathered and fast.

Deinonychus

North American predator known for its pack hunting and distinctive claw.

Utahraptor

Largest known dromaeosaurid; robust and powerful predator with huge sickle claw.
