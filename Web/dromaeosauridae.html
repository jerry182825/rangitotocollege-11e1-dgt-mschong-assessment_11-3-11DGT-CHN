Dromaeosauridae

Dromaeosauridae is a family of feathered theropod dinosaurs that lived from the Late Jurassic to the Late Cretaceous. They are known for their sickle-shaped claw on the second toe, agile bodies, and likely carnivorous diet. Fossils have been discovered mainly in North America, Europe, and Asia.

Featured — Velociraptor

One of the most famous dromaeosaurids; known for its sickle claw, feathered body, and predatory behavior.

Time: Late Cretaceous (≈ 75–71 million years ago)
Region: Asia
Diet: Carnivore

Typical size: Length ~2 m; Estimated mass ~15–20 kg.

Notable genera in Dromaeosauridae

Velociraptor

Small, agile predator with sickle-shaped claw; feathered and highly intelligent.

Deinonychus

Large dromaeosaurid from North America; famous for hunting in packs and sickle claw.

Utahraptor

One of the largest dromaeosaurids; powerful predator with robust sickle claw.
