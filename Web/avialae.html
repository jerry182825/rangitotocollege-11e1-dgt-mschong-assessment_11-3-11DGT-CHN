Avialae

Avialae is a clade of bird-like dinosaurs that includes all modern birds and their closest extinct relatives. They lived from the Late Jurassic to the present. Avialans are characterized by feathers, a furcula (wishbone), and adaptations for flight, though some early members were likely flightless or gliding. Fossils have been found worldwide.

Featured — Archaeopteryx

The most famous early avialan; known for its mix of bird-like and dinosaurian features, including feathers and teeth.

Time: Late Jurassic (≈ 150 million years ago)
Region: Europe
Diet: Carnivore / Insectivore

Typical size: Length ~0.5 m; Estimated mass ~0.5–1 kg.

Notable genera in Avialae

Archaeopteryx

Early avialan with teeth and feathers; transitional form between non-avian dinosaurs and birds.

Confuciusornis

Early Cretaceous avialan from China; toothless beak and long tail feathers.

Ichthyornis

Late Cretaceous avialan; toothed seabird showing modern bird-like wing and skeletal features.
