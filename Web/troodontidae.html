Troodontidae

Troodontidae is a family of small, bird-like theropod dinosaurs that lived from the Late Jurassic to the Late Cretaceous. They are known for their large brains, keen vision, sickle-shaped second toe claw, and likely omnivorous or carnivorous diets. Fossils have been found mainly in Asia and North America.

Featured — Troodon

One of the best-known troodontids; noted for its large brain, keen senses, and predatory or omnivorous behavior.

Time: Late Cretaceous (≈ 77–70 million years ago)
Region: North America
Diet: Omnivore / Carnivore

Typical size: Length ~2 m; Estimated mass ~40–50 kg.

Notable genera in Troodontidae

Troodon

Small, intelligent theropod with keen senses and a sickle-shaped claw; possibly omnivorous.

Saurornithoides

Asian troodontid; known for slender body, large eyes, and advanced brain size.

Mei

Small, feathered troodontid from China; notable for its sleeping posture and rapid agility.
