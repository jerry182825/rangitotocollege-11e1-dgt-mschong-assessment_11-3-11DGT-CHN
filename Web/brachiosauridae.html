Brachiosauridae

Brachiosauridae is a family of large, long-necked sauropod dinosaurs from the Late Jurassic to Early Cretaceous. They are known for their distinctive long forelimbs, high shoulders, and relatively upright necks. Fossils have been found mainly in North America, Africa, and Europe.

Featured — Brachiosaurus

The most representative brachiosaurid; famous for its long forelimbs and towering neck.

Time: Late Jurassic (≈ 154–150 million years ago)
Region: North America
Diet: Herbivore / browser

Typical size: Length ~25 m; Estimated mass ~35–56 tons.

Notable genera in Brachiosauridae

Brachiosaurus

Giant sauropod with long forelimbs, upright neck, and massive size; fossils mainly from North America.

Giraffatitan

African brachiosaurid; similar body plan to Brachiosaurus but slightly lighter and taller.

Cedarosaurus

North American genus; illustrates brachiosaurid limb proportions and vertebral anatomy.

Other representative genera (examples)
