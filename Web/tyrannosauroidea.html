Tyrannosauroidea

Tyrannosauroidea is a superfamily of large theropod dinosaurs that lived from the Late Jurassic to the Late Cretaceous. They are characterized by massive skulls, strong hindlimbs, tiny forelimbs with two fingers, and powerful jaws. Fossils have been found in North America, Asia, and Europe.

Featured — Tyrannosaurus

The most iconic tyrannosauroid; famous for its massive skull, powerful bite, and tiny two-fingered forelimbs.

Time: Late Cretaceous (≈ 68–66 million years ago)
Region: North America
Diet: Carnivore

Typical size: Length ~12–13 m; Estimated mass ~8–10 tons.

Notable genera in Tyrannosauroidea

Tyrannosaurus

Massive Late Cretaceous predator; apex carnivore of North America with extremely powerful jaws.

Albertosaurus

Medium-sized tyrannosauroid from North America; illustrates diversity within Tyrannosauroidea.

Tarbosaurus

Asian tyrannosauroid closely related to Tyrannosaurus; large skull and robust body.
