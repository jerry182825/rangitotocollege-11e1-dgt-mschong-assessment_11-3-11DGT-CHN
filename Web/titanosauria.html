Titanosauria

Titanosauria is a diverse group of large, long-necked sauropod dinosaurs that lived from the Late Jurassic to the end of the Cretaceous. They are characterized by wide hips, massive tails, and often armored bodies. Fossils have been found on every continent, with especially abundant finds in South America.

Featured — Titanosaurus

One of the most iconic titanosaurs; known for its massive body and long neck.

Time: Late Cretaceous (≈ 100–66 million years ago)
Region: South America
Diet: Herbivore / browser

Typical size: Length ~12–30 m; Estimated mass ~10–70 tons.

Notable genera in Titanosauria

Titanosaurus

Classic titanosaur; massive body, long tail and neck; fossils mainly from India and South America.

Argentinosaurus

One of the largest known dinosaurs; giant titanosaur from South America.

Saltasaurus

Small, armored titanosaur from South America; illustrates diversity within the group.

Other representative genera (examples)

Example — Titanosaurus indicus

Original titanosaur species; shows typical skeletal features.

Example — Argentinosaurus huinculensis

Giant titanosaur; largest known dinosaur by mass and length.

Example — Saltasaurus loricatus

Armored titanosaur; demonstrates smaller size and body diversity in the group.
