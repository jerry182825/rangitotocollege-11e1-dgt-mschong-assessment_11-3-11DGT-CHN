Ceratosauria

Ceratosauria is a group of early theropod dinosaurs that lived from the Late Triassic to Late Cretaceous. They are characterized by robust skulls, often with horn-like structures, and powerful jaws. Fossils have been found in North America, Europe, Africa, and South America.

Featured — Ceratosaurus

The most representative ceratosaur; known for its prominent nasal horn and carnivorous build.

Time: Late Jurassic (≈ 155–145 million years ago)
Region: North America
Diet: Carnivore

Typical size: Length ~5–6 m; Estimated mass ~400–700 kg.

Notable genera in Ceratosauria

Ceratosaurus

Medium-sized theropod with a prominent nasal horn and sharp teeth; fossils mainly from North America.

Abelisaurus

Late Cretaceous ceratosaur from South America; short snout and robust skull.

Majungasaurus

Armored skull, short snout; Cretaceous predator from Madagascar, shows diversity of ceratosaurs.

Other representative genera (examples)

Example — Ceratosaurus nasicornis

Classic Ceratosaurus species; shows distinctive horn and skeletal structure.

Example — Abelisaurus comahuensis

South American ceratosaur; robust skull, short snout, characteristic of abelisaurids.

Example — Majungasaurus crenatissimus

Madagascar ceratosaur; illustrates diversity and evolutionary trends of the group.
