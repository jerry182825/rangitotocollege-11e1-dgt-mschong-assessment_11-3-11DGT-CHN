Allosauroidea

Allosauroidea is a superfamily of large theropod dinosaurs that lived from the Middle Jurassic to the Late Cretaceous. They are characterized by large skulls with sharp teeth, strong hindlimbs, and often short forelimbs with three-fingered hands. Fossils have been found in North America, Europe, Asia, and Africa.

Featured — Allosaurus

The most representative allosauroid; known for its predatory build and large skull with serrated teeth.

Time: Late Jurassic (≈ 155–145 million years ago)
Region: North America
Diet: Carnivore

Typical size: Length ~8–12 m; Estimated mass ~1–2 tons.

Notable genera in Allosauroidea

Allosaurus

Large predator with a robust skull and sharp teeth; one of the most famous Jurassic theropods.

Carcharodontosaurus

Giant allosauroid from Africa; known for enormous skull and blade-like teeth.

Neovenator

Medium-sized European allosauroid; shows diversity and evolutionary trends of the group.
