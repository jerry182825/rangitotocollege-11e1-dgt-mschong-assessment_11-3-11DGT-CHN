Megalosauroidea

Megalosauroidea is a superfamily of large, basal theropod dinosaurs that lived from the Middle Jurassic to the Early Cretaceous. They were generally carnivorous with robust skulls, powerful jaws, and strong hindlimbs. Fossils have been found in Europe, North America, Africa, and Asia.

Featured — Megalosaurus

The most representative megalosauroid; known for its large size and predatory build.

Time: Middle Jurassic (≈ 166–161 million years ago)
Region: Europe
Diet: Carnivore

Typical size: Length ~6–9 m; Estimated mass ~500–1000 kg.

Notable genera in Megalosauroidea

Megalosaurus

Early large theropod; robust skull and strong limbs; one of the first dinosaurs scientifically described.

Torvosaurus

Giant carnivore from North America and Europe; demonstrates typical megalosauroid features.

Afrovenator

Medium-sized theropod from Africa; shows diversity and wide distribution of megalosauroids.
