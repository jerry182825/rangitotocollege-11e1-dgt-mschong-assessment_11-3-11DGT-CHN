Protoceratopsidae

Protoceratopsidae is a family of small to medium-sized, herbivorous ceratopsian dinosaurs that lived during the Late Cretaceous. They are characterized by beaked mouths, frills at the back of the skull, short quadrupedal bodies, and adaptations for plant-eating. Fossils have been found mainly in Asia, especially Mongolia and China.

Featured — Protoceratops

The most well-known protoceratopsid; small herbivore with a prominent frill and beaked mouth.

Time: Late Cretaceous (≈ 83–70 million years ago)
Region: Asia
Diet: Herbivore

Typical size: Length ~2 m; Estimated mass ~20–40 kg.

Notable genera in Protoceratopsidae

Protoceratops

Small herbivorous ceratopsian; notable for frilled skull and beaked mouth.

Bagaceratops

Early Cretaceous protoceratopsid from Mongolia; small frilled skull and quadrupedal body.

Montanoceratops

Small to medium ceratopsian; herbivorous, short frill, bipedal/quadrupedal adaptations.
