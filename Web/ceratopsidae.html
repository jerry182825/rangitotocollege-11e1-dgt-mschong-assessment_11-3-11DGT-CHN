Ceratopsidae

Ceratopsidae is a family of large, herbivorous ceratopsian dinosaurs that lived during the Late Cretaceous. They are characterized by large frills at the back of the skull, prominent facial horns, robust quadrupedal bodies, and adaptations for processing tough plant material. Fossils have been found mainly in North America and Asia.

Featured — Triceratops

The most famous ceratopsid; large herbivore with three facial horns and a prominent frill.

Time: Late Cretaceous (≈ 68–66 million years ago)
Region: North America
Diet: Herbivore

Typical size: Length ~8–9 m; Estimated mass ~6–12 tons.

Notable genera in Ceratopsidae

Triceratops

Large North American ceratopsid; three facial horns and a wide frill for defense and display.

Chasmosaurus

Medium-sized ceratopsid with long frill openings and horns; herbivorous and quadrupedal.

Pachyrhinosaurus

Large ceratopsid; thickened nasal boss instead of prominent horns, with frilled skull and herbivorous adaptations.
