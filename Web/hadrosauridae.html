Hadrosauridae

Hadrosauridae is a family of large, herbivorous "duck-billed" dinosaurs that lived from the Late Cretaceous. They are characterized by broad, flattened snouts, complex dental batteries for grinding plants, and often elaborate cranial crests for display or communication. Fossils have been found mainly in North America, Europe, and Asia.

Featured — Edmontosaurus

One of the best-known hadrosaurids; large herbivore with broad, duck-like snout and complex teeth for grinding vegetation.

Time: Late Cretaceous (≈ 73–66 million years ago)
Region: North America
Diet: Herbivore

Typical size: Length ~12 m; Estimated mass ~4–5 tons.

Notable genera in Hadrosauridae

Edmontosaurus

Large North American hadrosaurid; broad snout, complex teeth, and herbivorous adaptations.

Lambeosaurus

Hadrosaurid with a distinctive hollow cranial crest, likely used for vocalization and display.

Parasaurolophus

Late Cretaceous hadrosaurid from North America; known for its long, tubular cranial crest and herbivorous diet.
