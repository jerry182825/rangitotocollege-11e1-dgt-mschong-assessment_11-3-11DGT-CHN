Coelophysoidea

Coelophysoidea is a superfamily of early theropod dinosaurs that lived from the Late Triassic to Early Jurassic. They were generally small, bipedal carnivores with slender bodies and long tails. Fossils have been found in North America, Europe, and Africa.

Featured — Coelophysis

The most representative coelophysoid; known for its lightweight frame and agility.

Time: Late Triassic (≈ 225–200 million years ago)
Region: North America
Diet: Carnivore

Typical size: Length ~2–3 m; Estimated mass ~15–30 kg.

Notable genera in Coelophysoidea

Coelophysis

Small, agile theropod; abundant fossils from North America; slender skull and body.

Procompsognathus

Early small theropod from Europe; shows typical coelophysoid features.

Syntarsus (Megapnosaurus)

Small, lightly built carnivore from Africa; illustrates diversity in early theropods.
