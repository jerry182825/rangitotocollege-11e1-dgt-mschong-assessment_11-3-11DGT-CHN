Riojasauridae

Riojasauridae is a family of early, primarily herbivorous sauropodomorph dinosaurs that lived during the Late Triassic. They were large, quadrupedal or facultatively bipedal, and provide key insights into the early evolution of sauropodomorphs toward the giant sauropods of later periods. Fossils have been mainly found in South America, particularly Argentina.

Featured — Riojasaurus

The most well-known member of Riojasauridae; notable for its large size and robust limb bones.

Time: Late Triassic (≈ 227–201 million years ago)
Region: South America (Argentina)
Diet: Herbivore / browser

Typical size: Length ~6–10 m; Estimated mass ~1.5–2.5 tons. Shows relatively heavy, stocky limbs adapted for a quadrupedal gait.

Notable genera in/near Riojasauridae

Riojasaurus

Large quadrupedal sauropodomorph from Argentina; well-preserved skeletons provide insights into early sauropodomorph anatomy.

Eucnemesaurus

From South America; related to Riojasaurus, showing robust limbs and a heavy build.

Jingshanosaurus

A genus from China sometimes placed near Riojasauridae; provides comparison for geographic distribution and evolution.
