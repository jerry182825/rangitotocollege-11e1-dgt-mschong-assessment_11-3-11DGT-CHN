Stegosauria

Stegosauria is a clade of herbivorous, armored dinosaurs that lived from the Late Jurassic to the Early Cretaceous. They are characterized by rows of bony plates along the back, spiked tails (thagomizers), small heads, and a bulky body. Fossils have been found mainly in North America, Europe, and Asia.

Featured — Stegosaurus

The most famous stegosaur; known for its large dorsal plates, spiked tail, and herbivorous diet.

Time: Late Jurassic (≈ 155–150 million years ago)
Region: North America
Diet: Herbivore

Typical size: Length ~9 m; Estimated mass ~4–5 tons.

Notable genera in Stegosauria

Stegosaurus

Large plated herbivore with tail spikes (thagomizer); iconic Jurassic dinosaur.

Kentrosaurus

Small to medium stegosaur from Africa; tail spikes and small plates along the back.

Tuojiangosaurus

Chinese stegosaur with double row of plates and long spiked tail for defense.
