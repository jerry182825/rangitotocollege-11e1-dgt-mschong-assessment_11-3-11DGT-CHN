Ankylosauria

Ankylosauria is a clade of heavily armored, herbivorous dinosaurs that lived from the Late Jurassic to the Late Cretaceous. They are characterized by extensive body armor, low-slung bodies, wide hips, and often tail clubs for defense. Fossils have been found worldwide, including North America, Europe, and Asia.

Featured — Ankylosaurus

The most famous ankylosaur; known for its heavy armor and large tail club used for defense.

Time: Late Cretaceous (≈ 68–66 million years ago)
Region: North America
Diet: Herbivore

Typical size: Length ~6–8 m; Estimated mass ~4–6 tons.

Notable genera in Ankylosauria

Ankylosaurus

Largest ankylosaur with a massive tail club and heavily armored body.

Euoplocephalus

Medium ankylosaur from North America; broad body and extensive dorsal armor.

Pinacosaurus

Smaller Asian ankylosaur; notable for armor plates and tail club development.
