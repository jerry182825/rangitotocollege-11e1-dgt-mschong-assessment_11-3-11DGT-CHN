Psittacosauridae

Psittacosauridae is a family of small, herbivorous ceratopsian dinosaurs that lived during the Early Cretaceous. They are characterized by parrot-like beaks, bipedal posture, long tails, and in some species, simple quill-like structures on the tail. Fossils have been found mainly in Asia.

Featured — Psittacosaurus

The most well-known psittacosaurid; small herbivore with a parrot-like beak and bipedal stance.

Time: Early Cretaceous (≈ 126–101 million years ago)
Region: Asia
Diet: Herbivore

Typical size: Length ~2 m; Estimated mass ~20–25 kg.

Notable genera in Psittacosauridae

Psittacosaurus

Small bipedal ceratopsian; parrot-like beak, long tail, herbivorous diet.

Hongshanosaurus

Early Cretaceous psittacosaurid from China; similar body plan with some cranial variations.

Jeholosaurus

Small basal ceratopsian; closely related to psittacosaurids with bipedal posture and herbivorous adaptations.
